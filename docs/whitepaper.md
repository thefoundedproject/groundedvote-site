## Abstract

GroundedVote is a civic alignment engine that matches voters to candidates based on genuine policy proximity rather than party identity, media narrative, or name recognition. This paper documents the technical architecture, scoring methodology, evidence pipeline, and ethical design principles underlying the platform.

---

## 1. Mission and Framing

Democracy works when voters are informed. The current information environment rewards tribal loyalty and punishes nuance. GroundedVote exists to restore the signal: to surface which candidate's actual record and stated positions most closely matches what a voter says they believe.

The platform does not endorse candidates. It does not filter by party. It does not accept advertising. It is a transparency tool ГўВЂВ” a mirror held up to the political landscape.

## 3. Quiz Architecture

### 3.1 Question Generation

Questions are generated per-race by the bias audit pipeline (`lib/bias-audit.js`). Each question:

- Is framed on a 5-point Likert scale (Strongly Oppose ГўВ†В’ Strongly Support)
- Is topic-tagged (economy, healthcare, immigration, gun policy, environment, etc.)
- Undergoes a two-pass bias audit before being approved for production:
  - **Pass 1 (Fable):** High-quality inference to flag ideologically loaded framing
  - **Pass 2:** Structural checks ГўВЂВ” no leading language, no false premises, balanced terminology

Only questions with `auditStatus: 'APPROVED'` are served to voters.

### 3.2 Issue Salience

Before the policy quiz, voters complete a 6-question salience survey indicating which issues matter most to them (1ГўВЂВ“5 scale). These importance weights amplify matching scores on the issues the voter cares about most.

## 4. Scoring Algorithm

*Source: `lib/matching.js`*

### 4.1 Base Similarity

For each question, the proximity between a voter's answer and a candidate's answer is:

```
similarity = 1 - |userAnswer - candidateAnswer| / 4
```

This produces a score from 0.0 (maximum disagreement) to 1.0 (perfect alignment).

### 4.2 Importance Multiplier

The voter's stated importance for a topic amplifies or discounts each question's weight:

```
importanceMult = importanceLevel / 3  // 0.33x to 1.67x
effectiveWeight = question.weight ГѓВ— importanceMult
```

### 4.3 Discriminative Weighting

Questions where candidates cluster together (e.g., all score 4) carry little alignment information. Questions where candidates genuinely diverge (e.g., one scores 2, another scores 5) are the most diagnostic.

The discriminative multiplier amplifies ideologically differentiating questions:

```
spread = max(candidateAnswers) - min(candidateAnswers)   // 0 to 4
discriminativeMult = 1 + spread / 4                      // 1.0ГѓВ— to 2.0ГѓВ—
effectiveWeight = question.weight ГѓВ— importanceMult ГѓВ— discriminativeMult
```

This means a question where a moderate Democrat (3) faces a progressive Democrat (5) gets 1.5ГѓВ— weight, while a question where both candidates agree gets 1.0ГѓВ—. A maximum-spread question (1 vs 5) gets 2.0ГѓВ—.

### 4.4 Final Score

```
weightedSum = ГЋВЈ (similarity ГѓВ— effectiveWeight)
totalWeight = ГЋВЈ effectiveWeight
score = (weightedSum / totalWeight) ГѓВ— 100
```

Candidates are ranked by score. Higher score = closer alignment to the voter.

## 5. Candidate Position Pipeline

*Source: `lib/candidate-positions.js`*

Candidate answers are generated by an AI pipeline that pulls from multiple evidence sources, ranked by reliability.

|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | VoteSmart Political Courage Test | 0.90ГўВЂВ“0.98 | Self-reported by candidate ГўВЂВ” highest trust |
| 2 | VoteSmart Interest Group Ratings | 0.75ГўВЂВ“0.88 | ACU, ADA, AFL-CIO, NRA, Sierra Club, NARAL, ACLU, Heritage Action, Club for Growth, Planned Parenthood, US Chamber |
| 3 | Congress.gov Voting Record | 0.85ГўВЂВ“0.95 | Explicit votes and sponsored legislation (incumbents with bioguideId) |

### 5.4 AI Scoring Prompt Design

The AI model receives the full evidence context and outputs:

```json
{
  "answerValue": 3,
  "confidence": 0.82,
  "sourceNote": "VoteSmart PCT shows candidate supports border security but opposes mass deportation.",
  "evidenceType": "VOTESMART_PCT"
}
```

Critical prompt constraints:
- **Full range enforcement:** Moderates score 3ГўВЂВ“4, not 5. Reserve 5 for candidates who have sponsored legislation or made an issue a centerpiece campaign platform.
- **Evidence over party:** Party affiliation does not override direct evidence from record.
- **Honest confidence:** Confidence is shown to voters as a transparency signal. It must reflect actual evidence quality.

## 7. Bias Audit Framework

All questions pass through a structured audit before deployment.

**Audit principles:**
- No ideologically loaded framing (e.g., "job-killing regulations" vs. "vital environmental protections")
- No false binary premises
- Balanced terminology across the political spectrum
- Questions must be answerable from a candidate's public record

**Audit statuses:** `PENDING` ГўВ†В’ `APPROVED` / `REJECTED` / `NEEDS_REVISION`

Only `APPROVED` questions are served to voters and used in matching.

## 9. Ethical Design Principles

1. **No party favoritism.** The algorithm has no knowledge of which candidate is Republican or Democrat. Scores are derived entirely from policy proximity.

2. **Confidence transparency.** Every candidate answer shown to voters includes a confidence level. Voters can see when a score is based on a self-reported PCT vs. party inference.

3. **No advertising.** GroundedVote does not accept paid placement or advertising of any kind in its matching engine.

4. **Evidence primacy.** Direct evidence from a candidate's voting record or self-reported positions always overrides party-based inference.

5. **Moderate calibration.** The AI scoring prompt explicitly guards against over-scoring moderates. A senator who voted for a watered-down version of a policy scores 3ГўВЂВ“4, not 5.

6. **Civic purpose.** The tool exists to serve voters, not campaigns. Output is always framed as "your alignment score" ГўВЂВ” framed as a recommendation or endorsement.

## 11. Glossary

**Alignment score:** A percentage (0ГўВЂВ“100) representing how closely a voter's policy answers match a given candidate's record.

**Discriminative weighting:** A multiplier applied to questions where candidates genuinely differ. Questions where all candidates cluster together receive 1.0ГѓВ—; maximum-divergence questions receive 2.0ГѓВ—.

**Evidence type:** A code indicating the source of a candidate's position score (VoteSmart PCT, voting record, Ballotpedia, etc.).

**Issue salience:** The voter's stated importance level for a topic. Used as an amplifier in the matching algorithm.

**Political Courage Test (PCT):** VoteSmart's candidate questionnaire, in which candidates self-report their positions on policy issues. Considered the highest-confidence source in the GroundedVote evidence chain.

**Spread:** The range between the highest and lowest candidate scores on a given question. A spread of 4 (one candidate at 1, another at 5) produces the maximum discriminative multiplier.
